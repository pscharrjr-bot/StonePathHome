What credit score do you need for a mortgage?

There is no single magic credit score for every mortgage, but your score can strongly influence the options and pricing available to you.

What credit score do you need for a mortgage?

Mortgage qualification depends on more than one number, but credit score still matters because it affects loan eligibility, rate offers, and sometimes the total monthly payment.

Why score matters

A stronger credit profile can help a borrower access better rate options. Even a modest rate difference can change affordability meaningfully over the life of a mortgage.

Why there is no single answer

Different loan programs and lenders use different guidelines. Income, debt, cash reserves, down payment size, and the overall application also matter.

Planning takeaway

If your credit is improving, it may be worth comparing how different rate assumptions affect the payment before deciding what home price feels safe. StonePathHome calculators are helpful for testing those scenarios.

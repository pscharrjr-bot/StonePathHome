What is PITI?

PITI is one of the core mortgage acronyms buyers should understand because it helps explain why the real monthly payment is often more than principal and interest alone.

What is PITI?

PITI stands for principal, interest, taxes, and insurance. These are four of the major components that often shape the real monthly mortgage payment. Buyers who understand PITI usually have a much easier time making realistic affordability decisions.

What each letter means

  • P = principal
  • I = interest
  • T = property taxes
  • I = homeowners insurance

Why it matters

A lot of home buyers hear a principal-and-interest payment and assume that is the full monthly number. PITI is the reminder that taxes and insurance often need to be included too.

What PITI does not always include

PITI is helpful, but it still may not cover every housing cost. Depending on the loan and property, the full monthly payment may also include PMI or HOA dues.
